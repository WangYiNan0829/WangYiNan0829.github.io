About

Valery Wang

I first began taking photos casually with my phone in elementary school, recording food, weather, and small moments in daily life. In middle school, my interest became more intentional when I started photographing objects related to novels I loved—capturing things that mattered to me, even if I didn't yet think of it as "photography."

In high school, photography became part of how I connected with people and spaces. I started exploring cafés and streets, not just as locations, but as places where I could slow down and observe. I became president of the photography club, where I organize weekly activities, teach techniques, and lead collaborative projects like short films and campus photoshoots.

Photography has also allowed me to contribute to my community. I've photographed events for other clubs, helped organize a charity sale featuring student work, and taken instant portraits that gave classmates tangible memories to keep. What started as a personal practice has become a way of seeing, remembering, and connecting with others.
