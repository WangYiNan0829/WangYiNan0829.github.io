Artist Statement

Photography is both a way of observing the world and a way of understanding myself.

It began as something simple—recording meals, weather, and small moments with my phone. Over time, it became more intentional. In middle school, I photographed objects related to novels I loved. In high school, my focus shifted to cafés, streets, and the spaces where I felt most present. These aren't just locations; they're places where I slow down, observe, and reconnect with my emotions.

My work reflects my state of mind. When life feels calm, I seek out quiet corners and familiar streets. When I'm struggling, I lose the desire to explore. Photography has taught me that my ability to notice beauty is tied to my emotional well-being—and that creating images is a way of processing how I feel.

I approach each photograph with intention. I research locations, plan for light, and think about what I want to express. But I also leave room for spontaneity—for the unexpected moments that reveal something true.

Through the photography club and community events, I've learned that photography can also be a gift to others. When classmates told me that the instant portraits I took at the charity sale helped them remember a joyful moment, I realized that my work could create meaning beyond my own experience. This recognition is both encouraging and humbling—it motivates me to keep improving while reminding me that photography is not just about technical skill, but about connection.

Photography has become inseparable from how I live. It's how I observe, how I remember, and how I share what I see with others. It's a practice that continues to teach me about patience, presence, and the quiet beauty of everyday life.
