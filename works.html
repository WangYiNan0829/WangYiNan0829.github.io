Selected Works

Everyday Spaces

Cafés, streets, and small corners where I slow down and observe. These places are not just locations—they are where I reconnect with myself and rediscover the beauty in ordinary moments.

Light and Distance

Travel, sunsets, and the way light transforms familiar places into something new. I plan each shot carefully—researching locations, timing the light, and thinking about how space and emotion connect.

Hong Kong, 2025

This photograph holds special meaning for me. It was taken during a rare trip with my father during National Day—our first time traveling together in years. Our relationship had been distant, sometimes strained. But on this day, I set up the camera at the University of Hong Kong, and he pressed the shutter. The image shows my back, facing a place I'd always wanted to visit. For me, it represents more than a location—it's a quiet reconciliation, a memory of connection where there had been distance.

Objects and Small Stories

From novel merchandise in middle school to café details today, I've always been drawn to objects that carry memory and emotion. These small things tell quiet stories about what we love and how we remember.
