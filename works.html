Selected Works

Everyday Spaces

Cafés, streets, and small corners where I slow down and observe. These places are not just locations—they are where I reconnect with myself and rediscover the beauty in ordinary moments.

Light and Distance

Travel, sunsets, and the way light transforms familiar places into something new. I plan each shot carefully—researching locations, timing the light, and thinking about how space and emotion connect.

Hong Kong, 2025

The photo of a visit to Hong Kong is quite meaningful to me, and I still remember it very well. It was the first time I went out with my father in other places since I grew up. At that time, he had a holiday on the National Day, and he finally had time for the holiday. This photo was taken when I was traveling. At that time, I waited for a long time to take this photo, because there were a lot of people queuing up at that time. Although my father doesn't understand, he still stays with me. In my impression, the role of father is actually not very present. He often said that he sent me to school when he was a child, but I can't remember it now. Then, as I grew older, my relationship with my father became awkward and often quarreled. So this photo is also a memorial to the relaxation of my relationship with my father, so this photo is very meaningful to me.

Objects and Small Stories

From novel merchandise in middle school to café details today, I've always been drawn to objects that carry memory and emotion. These small things tell quiet stories about what we love and how we remember.
