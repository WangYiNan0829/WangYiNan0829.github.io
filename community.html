Community Through Photography

Photography has become more than a personal practice—it's how I connect with others, contribute to my school community, and help people preserve their memories.

Photography Club President

As president of the photography club, I organize weekly activities where we explore techniques, share creative ideas, and work on collaborative projects. We've created short films, organized campus photoshoots, and built a community of students who see photography as both art and storytelling.

Leading the club has taught me how to bring people together around a shared interest and create space for creative exploration.

Event Photography

I've collaborated with other school clubs to document their activities. For the badminton club, I photographed their competitions, capturing the intensity and joy of the matches. These photos became part of their club archive and helped them share their achievements with the wider school community.

This work showed me how photography can serve others—not just as documentation, but as a way to honor people's efforts and celebrate their moments.

Charity Sale & Instant Portraits

During our school's charity sale, I helped print and sell photography works created by club members. I also set up an instant photo station where I took portraits of students—photos they could take home immediately as physical keepsakes.

Many students told me afterward that these photos helped them remember the joy of that day. Hearing that my work gave them something tangible to hold onto was incredibly meaningful. It reminded me that photography isn't just about the image—it's about creating memories for others.
